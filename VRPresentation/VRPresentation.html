How to Start Using VR for Education?

Buying a VR Headset * ::

  • Apple Vision Pro: $3499 – $3899 (256GB – 1TB)

  • Galaxy XR (released 10/21/25): $1799 (512GB)

  • Meta-Quest 3/3S: $299 and $499 (128GB/512GB)

VR for Education: Investment

Meta-Quest 3/3S $299/$499 (128GB/512GB)

8 – 16 VR headsets plus 1 instructor headsets are needed:

  • for practical reasons, it is difficult to manage more than 16 students at a time in a classroom (take a TA if possible)
  • a VR experience can be delivered in two batches (allows class sizes between 16 and 32 students)

Total Cost 8+1 headsets (with GB128/GB256): $2,691 – $4,491

Total Cost 16+1 headsets (with GB128/GB256): $5,083 – $8,483

Student Preparation Check-List

  1. Ask about pre-existing conditions like epilepsy
  2. Inform students about possible vertigo and to stop early
  3. The browser version provides a good work-around for students who cannot use a VR headset
  4. Deliver the experience in sitting not standing mode
  5. Run headsets in tunnel-mode
  6. Introduce how to use controllers to move around and to select items
  7. Explain how to enter the world or to start the app

Classroom vs. VR-Lab vs. Online

Cloud based Horizon World requires high WiFi bandwidth

Work-Arounds:

  • VR-Lab provides high bandwidth
  • Work with groups of about 8 students
  • All or some students use their cell phone as a WiFi hotspot
  • Send some students (together with a TA) to another WiFi access point location
  • Teach the VR experience online

Alternative: Standalone Apps have no or low WiFi requirements
